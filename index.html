My Story

I've been obsessed with audio since I got my first 4-track recorder at 11 years old. Growing up in 90s Los Angeles as a musician, I spent a lot of time in clubs like The Roxy and Whiskey, quickly realizing I was more critical of sound than most people - I always felt like it could be better.

I learned early on that sound quality has a huge impact on how people connect with the source, whether music, film or an interview.

As a teenager, I interned at Village Recording Studios, seeing how real pros actually worked. Inspired, I began to explore more of the audio world, studying sound design in college and learning to mix sound for video.

My early obsession eventually led to real projects - PBS documentaries, live broadcast work, campaigns that raised serious money.

After a few years in the corporate world working on regenerative agriculture and supply chain initiatives, I eventually realized I needed to get back behind the console. Now I help businesses deliver audio that drives results.
